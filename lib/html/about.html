Bank of Parliament takes publicly available data, regarding the financial interests of politicians and visualises it in an easy to understand way.

Find out who received the most gifts, who owns the most property and who has the most lucrative private sector jobs.

The purpose of the website is to provide a convenient front end to view the publicly available data regarding the registered financial interests of members of parliament.

The parliamentary data is really just a starting point for a much deeper delve into the financial interests of members. Using varied sources such as companies house, expenses records and the land registry, the goal is to build a picture of each individuals interests and to then highlight any potential conflicts of interests, that the press and public may find interesting.

Disclaimer

At present, the data presented here in this website is not 100% accurate. There are instances where raw data entries are worded strangely, which has resulted in the incorrect parsing of the raw data.

Development is underway on better solutions to extract accurate data.
